Project
Optimization of Ferro Alloy in LRF

The ladle refining furnace (LRF) is crucial in steelmaking, refining molten steel from primary smelting furnaces. Its main functions include heating, alloy addition, composition adjustment, and slag incorporation for desulfurization and deoxidation. This project aims to optimize ferroalloy utilization in LRF operations by enhancing precision in alloy additions, reducing reliance on operator discretion, and addressing equipment reliability issues. By integrating data analytics and machine learning, we aim to develop predictive models to optimize ferroalloy quantities and combinations based on real-time steel chemistry. This will streamline operations, reduce costs, and ensure high-quality steel production.

About Ladle Refractory Furnece

A ladle refractory furnace is essential in steelmaking for holding and transporting molten steel between refining and casting stages. Its refractory lining provides thermal insulation and protects the ladle shell from extreme heat and corrosion. During refining, processes like deoxidation and alloy addition occur, with gases injected to stir the molten steel for uniformity. Regular maintenance of the refractory lining is crucial to ensure efficiency and prevent failures, making it vital for quality steel production.
